About Me

I am an undegraduate student at the University of Sao Paulo (USP) in Brazil. In September 2021, with the support of the PGSM Scholarship, I will move to Paris to obtain my Master Degree at Sorboonne University, in the same month I will turn 22 and Sally Rooney's new book will come out. Should be an interesting month. I completed my Senior Thesis at USP in 2020, where my advisor was Ivan Struchiner, we studied Morse Theory, and it's applications such as the H-Cobordism theorem, aiming for the proof of the Generalized Poincaré Conjecture. My mathematical interests are mostly in Differential Geometry, particularly the interaction of at least two of the following fields: Riemannian Geometry, Algebraic Topology and Analysis of PDEs. But I am always interested in learning about new areas and new problems.

My slides from some of my recent talks can be found here. Here is a copy of my current CV.

I also organize a student run academic group, called A5 Group. During my undergraduate years I also took two summer courses at IMPA. For some of my other non-mathematical interests, see the fun stuff section.
